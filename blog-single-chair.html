Memoirs of a chair

  • Nyamboks Fredrick
  • March 12, 2023

As I sit down to pen this essay, I must confess that I am feeling quite perturbed. You see, this is my inaugural foray into the world of essay writing, particularly as the esteemed chairman of the Vineyard Ministry.

To say that I am feeling a tad intimidated would be a gross understatement. However, I shall endeavor to put my best foot forward and imbue this piece with my vast lexicon.

You see, this is my inaugural foray into the world of essay writing, particularly as the esteemed chairman of the Vineyard Ministry.

As the curtains of life opened upon this leadership role, there I stood in the midst of my two-bedroom abode in hall 11, the protagonist of my own grandiose tale, ready to dazzle the vineyarders with my impeccable acting skills. Perhaps you've forgotten that William Shakespeare once said "All the world's a stage, and all the men and women merely players,". He must have had me in mind when penning those very words.

Detour.

At first, I was taken aback by Shakespeare. Me? A mere player in this grandiose production we call life? How dare he insinuate such a thing! But as I began to ponder the matter more deeply, I realized that old Bill was onto something. Like a thespian preparing for a performance, I carefully craft my persona to fit the role I am playing at any given moment. Whether I am portraying the confident Vineyard ministry chairman or the loving and attentive partner in my boyfriend duties, I am always performing.

The people you should focus on

Take heed now lest my onstage presence engulfs your attention, at the expense of other performers in this grandiose production, the vineyarders themselves. In this scene, they shall portray divas, clowns, heroes, and villains alike, exuding equal fervor. As my leadership reaches its third month today, most of the characters and roles ought to have been assigned. Although the antagonists may seemingly surpass the protagonists, our resolve remains unwavering, determined to execute our roles until the very denouement.

I must expatiate on my profound love for this ministry. This ministry stirs my passions.It is, in fact, one of the three things I love about the University of Nairobi, another one perhaps being Janet's chapati, which is unparalleled in its taste, texture and color. I wouldnt want to indulge you on the first one for now.

Love. I have often ruminated on the nature of love, but I must concede that I am no expert in this field. Yeah. My knowledge about love is as scarce as a hen's teeth. Indeed, one could argue that I know as much about love as a platypus knows about pole-vaulting. In one of my escapades in Baringo , being so oblivious to the ways of love, I once mistook a cactus for a rose and ended up with a dozen thorns and blisters in my hand.

The Vineyard we know

Nevertheless, I digress. Allow me to elucidate on our version of Vineyard Ministry . In this parched shamba la mawe of of city, Vineyard is a veritable oasis of spiritual nourishment . Our weekly meetings are a resplendent display of worship and fellowship, and our Bible study sessions are replete with erudite insights and cogent exegesis.

Ah, the Vineyard Ministry - a place of refuge for young and naive valors and even the old and terrible waldenses and Elects. It's a place where love abounds, where sick friends are amply visited, and where sundowners are the perfect excuse for Okidi to make terrible jokes. But don't let the warm fuzzies fool you - the Vineyard Ministry is not without its quirks.

For starters like you who thinks everything is going on very well, there's the issue of attendance. The Chair is often left disappointed when members fail to show up to meetings, leaving him to twiddle his thumbs and wonder where he went wrong. It's not that the vineyarders aren't dedicated - they just have a penchant for being fashionably late. Or not showing up at all. Whoops.

And then there's the Vineyard WhatsApp group. Here, we see big things my friend. Your esteemed Chair can often be found waxing lyrical about the latest pledges for the PA system or the forthcoming children's home event, only to be met with a deafening silence. Again it's not that the vineyarders aren't interested - they just have a healthy respect for the sound of crickets chirping in the group. After all, silence is golden.

Our goals for the ministry

The Vineyard Ministry we are asking God to build is a place of community where togetherness is consistently on offer. It's a place where the sojourners can come together to share their faith, and the millerites, their struggles and hopes for the future. And when the seraphims are not busy failing to show up to meetings or ignoring the Chair's messages, we hope to find them out and about, exploring the world and attending spitual events in other ministries. After all, life is meant to be lived - and the Vineyard Ministry is proof that you can still have fun while following the word of God.

So here's to the Vineyard Ministry - a place where love, laughter, and a healthy dose of tardiness reign supreme. It may not be perfect, but it's a community that's bound together by their faith and their dedication to a fellow vineyarder. And that's something to be celebrated, even if it means occasionally disappointing the Chair or remaining eerily quiet in the WhatsApp group. After all, nobody's perfect - but the Vineyard Ministry comes pretty close.

In closing, I must admit that this essay has been quite a daunting task. However, I take solace in the fact that I have utilized a plethora of sesquipedalian words to express some deep feelings and fears, which should surely impress even the most discerning of readers. And so, I bid you adieu, dear reader, and I implore you to join us at the Vineyard Ministry for a transcendental experience like no other while we journey here below or to meet us at the Vineyard corner in the city four-square.
